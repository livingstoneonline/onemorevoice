<h4 style="text-align:center;">The Church Missionary Gleaner</h4>
<br />
<h5 style="text-align:center;">May, 1851</h5>
<br />
<h6 style="text-align:center;"><em>Abbeokuta's Resolution.</em></h6>
<div class="poem">
<div class="stanza">
<div Class="lineno">
They come at length, a rabble rout,</div><div class="lineno">
We hear afar their noisy shout:</div><div class="lineno">
The robbers come in eager haste,</div><div class="lineno">
Our country and its homes to waste.</div><div class="lineno">
Like storm-clouds rising on the blast</div><div class="lineno">
A momentary gloom they cast—</div><div class="lineno">
The hurricane will soon have past.</div><div class="lineno">
What though our enemies be near,</div><div class="lineno">
The Yorubas disdain to fear:</div><div class="lineno"> 
Our children and our wives are dear.</div><div class="lineno">
For those we peril precious life,</div><div class="lineno">
And mingle in the bloody strife,</div><div class="lineno">
Father for child, husband for wife,</div><div class="lineno">
Until the tyrant Gezo knows</div><div class="lineno">
He has not women for his foes.</div><div class="lineno">
</div></div></div>
