One More Voice

Introduction

Welcome to One More Voice, a digital humanities recovery project that identifies, documents, and critically engages with the voices of racialized creators in British imperial and colonial archives. The voices take multiple forms and appear in multiple genres. Our project seeks to introduce these rich and diverse materials to broad academic and public audiences. Recourse to the voices promises to transform our understanding of imperial and colonial history and literature while foregrounding perspectives that scholarship in majority has hitherto overlooked or silenced.

Lead image commentary: The One More Voice home page image plays several roles in the context of the site. As the lead image of the site’s “solidarity” edition (2022), the image alludes to the fact that the work of One More Voice is fundamentally relational and that the project's main efforts center on collaboration with individuals and other projects. The image, which represents the work of a local West African artist, also points to the roots of One More Voice in African Studies and to the fact that a large part of the site’s recovered texts are by African creators. The textile’s colors, additionally, have set the basis of the One More Voice site colors as a whole, thereby allowing the site – throughout its pages – to underscore visually and symbolically its efforts to honor, restore, and foreground the work of creators historically excluded from English literary studies. Finally, the choice of leading with an image from a repository centered on cultures of a region in the Global South – the Smithsonian’s National Museum of African Art – reflects the intentionality with which One More Voice seeks to direct its work against dominant Eurocentric narratives of history and creativity.
