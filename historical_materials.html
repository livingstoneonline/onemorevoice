Lead Image Details

Lead image caption: Image of a two-page spread from David Livingstone and Informants from Zanzibar, “Notebook III” (22 July-7 October 1871), in Livingstone Online, ed. Adrian S. Wisnicki and Megan Ward, new version, second edition (Lincoln, NE: University of Nebraska-Lincoln, 2020). Copyright David Livingstone Centre, Blantyre. Lead image commentary: The page at right, the first in a series of such pages, presents a list of Swahili words with definitions in English. Notably, the list begins with “Taone or Towny,” the Swahili word for “cholera.” Cholera had been the source of a significant epidemic in Zanzibar and East Africa in 1869 and 1870. The page at left makes clear that Livingstone worked with a variety of informants to gather the words in the list: “Suaheli spoken by the people from Zanzibar = collected in 1870-71.” The inclusion of such raw, field-based data points to the layered authorship of this document; word choices – for instance, the fact that the list begins with the words for “cholera” – indicate that local, non-European cultural priorities exerted more influence in shaping the list than might be evident at first.
