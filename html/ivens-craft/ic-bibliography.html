Overview

This bibliography assembles the diverse sources that this project engages and from which it gathers facts, inspiration, methodology, and stories. The bibliography is divided into three sections:

  • Primary Sources are those, generally drawn from William Ivens Craft’s lifetime, that offer insight into the world William Ivens Craft lived in, but are documents that require historical interpretation. They include manuscript sources, archival sources, newspaper clippings, and even printed books from the ninteenth and early twentieth centuries. The links given below for Ancestry.com, Newspapers.com, GenealogyBank, Gale, FamilySearch, and FindMyPast take you to the homepage for these resources, rather than the actual documents, because these websites require a paid subscription to access the documents.

  • Secondary Sources are those that offer scholarly interpretation, historical context, and critical insight. Most of the secondary sources engaged for this project consist of relatively recent and peer-reviewed scholarly books and articles.

  • Family Histories and Miscellaneous is a category meant to encompass a novel feature of this project: that it is based largely in part on and indeed has sought to engage fully the history passed down to the Craft family descendants. The items cited here include emails, Zoom meetings, and phone calls with members of the transatlantic Craft family diaspora. Their stories, input, and expertise have been crucial to piecing this narrative together.
