William Ivens' brother, Charles Craft, decided to build his family and home in Charleston, South Carolina--where is pictured here with his Emeline Kinloch Craft, and their daughter, Julia Ellen Craft. The brothers, Charles and William Ivens, worked together as agents on the Post Office Rail Service in the 1870s, yet likely had not seen each other since the early 1880s when William Ivens returned to the brothers' country of birth.

“Elizabeth Pease Nichol,” in The Life and Work of Susan B. Anthony, by Ida Husted Harper, vol. II (Indianapolis: The Bowen-Merrill Company, 1898), 568. Public domain. This image captured the likeness of Elizabeth Pease Nichol late in her life, offering a sense of what she might have looked like when she sat down to write her letters to and about William Ivens. That her portrait appeared in a book about famous suffragist Susan B. Anthony indicates that, by the last decades of her life, her suffrage work had overshadowed her legacy as an abolitionist.
